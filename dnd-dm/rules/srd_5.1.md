# D&D 5e rules reference

Mechanics summarized from the **System Reference Document 5.1** (CC-BY-4.0,
© Wizards of the Coast). Paraphrased for play, not verbatim. Each `##` section is
one retrievable chunk; append more sections to extend coverage (or drop in the
full SRD split the same way).

## Advantage and Disadvantage
Roll 2d20 and take the higher (advantage) or lower (disadvantage). They don't
stack — having advantage twice is still one extra die. If you have both
advantage and disadvantage from any number of sources, they cancel and you roll
one d20. Advantage/disadvantage are not added to other rolls; only one d20 is
kept.

## Making an Attack
Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient) vs the target's
Armor Class. A roll that equals or exceeds AC hits. A natural 20 always hits and
is a critical hit; a natural 1 always misses. On a critical hit, roll the
attack's damage dice twice and add them, then add relevant modifiers once.

## Cover
Half cover: +2 to AC and Dexterity saves (low wall, furniture, another creature).
Three-quarters cover: +5 to AC and Dexterity saves (arrow slit, tree trunk).
Total cover: can't be targeted directly. Cover bonuses don't stack; use the best.

## Conditions
Blinded: can't see, auto-fail sight checks; attacks against have advantage, its
attacks have disadvantage. Charmed: can't attack the charmer; charmer has
advantage on social checks. Deafened: can't hear, auto-fail hearing checks.
Frightened: disadvantage on checks and attacks while the source is in sight;
can't willingly move closer. Grappled: speed 0. Incapacitated: no actions or
reactions. Invisible: heavily obscured; attacks against have disadvantage, its
attacks have advantage. Paralyzed: incapacitated, can't move/speak, auto-fail
Str/Dex saves; attacks against have advantage and any hit within 5 ft is a crit.
Petrified: turned to solid substance, incapacitated, resistant to all damage.
Poisoned: disadvantage on attacks and ability checks. Prone: disadvantage on
attacks; melee attacks against have advantage, ranged against have disadvantage;
costs half movement to stand. Restrained: speed 0, disadvantage on attacks and
Dex saves; attacks against have advantage. Stunned: incapacitated, auto-fail
Str/Dex saves, attacks against have advantage. Unconscious: incapacitated,
prone, drops what it holds, auto-fail Str/Dex saves; attacks against have
advantage and any hit within 5 ft is a crit.

## Actions in Combat
On your turn you get one action, one bonus action (if available), and movement.
Actions: Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash (extra movement equal to speed), Disengage
(movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks), Dodge (attacks against you have
disadvantage; advantage on Dex saves), Help (give an ally advantage), Hide
(Stealth check), Ready (prepare an action with a trigger), Search, Use an Object.

## Opportunity Attacks
When a hostile creature you can see leaves your reach, you can use your reaction
to make one melee attack against it. Taking the Disengage action, or being moved
without using your own movement, avoids provoking.

## Ability Checks and Difficulty Class
Roll d20 + ability modifier (+ proficiency if proficient in the skill) vs a DC.
Typical DCs: very easy 5, easy 10, medium 15, hard 20, very hard 25, nearly
impossible 30. The DM sets the DC. Passive score = 10 + modifiers (used for
perception without rolling).

## Saving Throws
Roll d20 + ability modifier (+ proficiency if proficient in that save) vs an
effect's DC. A spell's save DC = 8 + caster's proficiency bonus + spellcasting
ability modifier.

## Dropping to 0 Hit Points and Death Saving Throws
At 0 HP a creature falls unconscious. On each of its turns it rolls a death save:
d20, 10+ is a success, 9- is a failure. Three successes = stable; three failures
= dead. A natural 20 = regain 1 HP; a natural 1 = two failures. Taking any damage
while at 0 HP = one failure (a crit or damage ≥ max HP = instant death). Any
healing brings it back to consciousness.

## Resting
Short rest: at least 1 hour; spend Hit Dice to heal (roll + Con mod per die).
Long rest: at least 8 hours; regain all HP and half your total Hit Dice, and
reset most daily abilities. One long rest per 24 hours.

## Resistance and Vulnerability
Resistance: take half damage of that type (round down). Vulnerability: take
double. Resistance and vulnerability to the same instance cancel. Apply
resistance/vulnerability after all other modifiers to that damage.

## Grappling and Shoving
Both are a special melee attack (part of the Attack action). The target contests
with an Athletics or Acrobatics check against your Athletics check. Grapple:
target's speed becomes 0. Shove: knock prone or push 5 feet. Target must be no
more than one size larger than you.

## Concentration
Some spells require concentration to maintain. You lose concentration if you cast
another concentration spell, are incapacitated, or die. Taking damage forces a
Constitution save (DC 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher) to keep
it.

## Unseen Attackers and Targets
Attacking a target you can't see: disadvantage (you guess its location). When a
creature can't see you, you have advantage on attacks against it. Being hidden
(unseen and unheard) grants advantage on your first attack, which then reveals
you.

## Spellcasting Basics
A spell is cast at its level or higher using a spell slot of that level or higher;
cantrips cost no slot. Most spells need verbal, somatic, and/or material
components — being unable to provide one (e.g. silenced, hands bound) blocks the
spell. Casting time is usually one action; some are bonus actions or reactions.
You can cast at most one leveled spell per turn if either is cast as a bonus
action. Slots refresh on a long rest.

## Spell Attacks and Saving Throws
Spell attack bonus = proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier; roll vs
the target's AC like any attack. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus +
spellcasting ability modifier; the target rolls the save the spell specifies.
Many damaging spells deal half damage on a successful save. Spellcasting ability
is Intelligence (wizard), Wisdom (cleric, druid, ranger), or Charisma (bard,
sorcerer, warlock, paladin).

## Areas of Effect
Spheres, cones, lines, and cubes affect every creature in the area. Each makes
its own saving throw where applicable. Total cover blocks an area of effect.
Common shapes: a cone's length equals its width at the far end; a line has a set
length and width; a sphere is measured by radius from a point you can see.

## Vision and Light (Obscurement)
Lightly obscured (dim light, light fog, foliage): disadvantage on Wisdom
(Perception) checks relying on sight. Heavily obscured (darkness, thick fog): you
are effectively blinded looking into it. Darkvision lets a creature see in dim
light as if bright, and in darkness as if dim (in shades of grey) out to a range.

## Exhaustion
Measured in six levels, each cumulative: 1 disadvantage on ability checks;
2 speed halved; 3 disadvantage on attacks and saves; 4 hit point maximum halved;
5 speed reduced to 0; 6 death. A long rest removes one level (if you've eaten and
drunk).

## Falling and Improvised Damage
Falling: 1d6 bludgeoning per 10 feet fallen, max 20d6, and you land prone (unless
you avoid the fall's damage). For effects without listed damage, gauge by
severity: setback ~1d10, dangerous ~2d10, deadly ~4d10+ (a burning building, a
pit of acid), scaling up to 24d6 for the most lethal hazards.
